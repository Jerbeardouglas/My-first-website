Perspectives Horizon Line

What is a horizon line? Horizon lines are used in sketches to represent the viewer's eye level. It serves as a crucial refrence point for creating depth and perspective in art.

In the context of landscapes, horizon points that are drawn near the bottom of the page focuses on the page usually focuses more on the detail of the sky, in architecture, a low horizon line would focus more on the desired building than on the street. A horizon line drawn near the top of the page emphesizes more on the objects below. A horizon line near the top can be used to draw rooftops or a more detailed street view. The image below describes the use of a high horizon line.
