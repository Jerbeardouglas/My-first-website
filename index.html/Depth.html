How to create depth in your sketches

Depth is enhancing the three-dimensionality of your artwork, making it look more enganging and realistic. Achieving depth allows dimensional space on a two dimensional surface. it allows the viewers to perceive foreground, middle ground, and background elements making your sketches look more immersive. But what is foreground, middle ground and background elements? Let's break it down.

1. Foreground

Foreground in sketching is the area closest to the viewer. Usually, the foreground contains elements that draw immediate attention. This place is often filled in detail and or color.

2. Middle ground

Middle ground is the area that is in between the foreground and background, it serves as a transitional space that connects the detailed foreground with the more generelized background. Middle ground area are usually less detailed than the foreground area.

3. Background

Background is the furthest area away from the viewer, these parts usually lack in detail but still play an important role in creating depth and dimension within a sketch. Backgrounds can be used to enhance the overall composition.
