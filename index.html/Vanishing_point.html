How to draw using vanishing points

Vanishing points are a crucial concept in perspective drawing, where parallel lines in a 3D space appear to converge to a single point on the horizon line, creating the illusion of depth on a 2D surface. Vanishing points are used to guide the viewer's eye, adding realism drama to architectural designs by depicting how objects appear to look smaller as they get farther away. There are many different types of perspective, theres 1 point, 2 point, 3 point and 4 point perspective. This section of the website will show you how to use these different types of perspectives. Vanishing points are made by marking the points on the horizon, then creating lines to complete the building you're trying to make.

1. One point perspective

One point perspective only uses one vanishing point on the horizon line. This gives the drawing a depth feeling. The picture below is an example of a one point perspective, it only focuses on the street and the walls of the building,

2. Two point perspective

Two point perspective uses two vanishing points. The two vanishing points allows the building or object to be seen on two sides, including the top or bottom of the object.

3. Three point perspective

Three point perspective involved the use of two points on the horizon and one point on the bottom, this allows the viewer to see the building in three perspectives.

4. Four point perspective

Four point perspective-unlike the other perspectives-uses a different method. The method involves drawing out a circle and sketching out curved lines horizontally and vertically, this will give us a sphere like shape in our sketch. It creates a more dynamic effect, by adding a fourth vanishing point, it gives a representation of wide scenes or objects. This perspective is often useful for depicting scenes with a wide field of view, distortion also appears.
